<p><p>Aero engine nacelles are critical structures that house aircraft engines, influencing aerodynamics and performance. The market is segmented into two main types: wing-hung nacelles and tail-hung nacelles. </p><p>Wing-hung nacelles are mounted under the wings, providing better aerodynamic efficiency and reducing drag, commonly seen in commercial aircraft. In contrast, tail-hung nacelles are designed to be attached to the rear fuselage, often found in military or specialized aircraft, offering design flexibility and weight distribution advantages. Both types serve distinct operational needs in aviation.</p></p>

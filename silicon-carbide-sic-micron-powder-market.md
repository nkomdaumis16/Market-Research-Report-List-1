<p><p>Silicon Carbide (SiC) micron powder is categorized into two primary types: α-SiC and β-SiC. α-SiC, known for its hexagonal crystal structure, is widely used in abrasive applications and high-performance ceramics due to its hardness and thermal stability. β-SiC, with a cubic crystal structure, offers higher electrical conductivity and is favored in semiconductor applications. Both types serve distinct purposes, catering to industries such as electronics, automotive, and aerospace, driving the demand for specialized SiC micron powders in advanced technologies.</p></p>

<p><p>Systemic amyloidosis encompasses different types based on its origins. Primary Systemic Amyloidosis (PSA) arises from abnormal protein production by plasma cells, often linked to conditions like multiple myeloma. Secondary Systemic Amyloidosis occurs due to chronic inflammatory diseases, where amyloid proteins develop as a response to ongoing inflammation, such as in rheumatoid arthritis. Other types may include hereditary forms or localized amyloidosis that seldom impacts systemic health. Each type requires tailored treatment and management strategies influenced by their underlying causes.</p></p>

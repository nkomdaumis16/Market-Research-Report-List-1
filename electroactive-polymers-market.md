<p><p>The electroactive polymers (EAP) market is primarily divided into two types: electronic and ionic. Electronic type EAPs respond to electric fields to produce mechanical motion or shape changes, commonly used in actuators, sensors, and displays. In contrast, ionic type EAPs operate through ion movement in a liquid or gel environment, enabling deformation when subjected to an electric field. Both types find applications across industries, including robotics, medical devices, and consumer electronics, due to their lightweight and adaptable characteristics.</p></p>
